Hello! My name is Sai Roop Somaraju, a Computer Engineering graduate student at Virginia Tech. My current research focuses on enhancing the eBPF subsystem within Linux kernels to improve safety, under the guidance of Dr. Dan Williams at the ROSA lab. Additionally, I'm also a part-time assistant to Prof. Carl Harris, teaching an undergraduate course on Network Applications Design.

Before joining Virginia Tech, I worked at a startup developing edge computing solutions for IoT devices. My career also includes a stint at Qualcomm as a firmware engineer, focusing on WLAN Hardware Abstraction and the PHY layer.

When I'm not engaged in programming, I enjoy backpacking in the woods, listening to music, and cooking.

If you're in search of an aspiring low-level developer with an interest in Systems Engineering, Firmware Engineering, or Network Engineering, feel free to email me at sairoop@vt.edu or connect with me on Linkedin.
